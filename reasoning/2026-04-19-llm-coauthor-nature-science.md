## Base rate

The reference class is "AI credited as author in top-tier scientific journal" which has a near-zero historical base rate. Specifics:

- **Nature's policy (January 2023):** "No LLM tool will be accepted as a credited author on a research paper." Reaffirmed through 2024–2025. Nature requires authors to take responsibility for the work, which an AI cannot.
- **Science's policy (January 2023):** AI-generated text without citation constitutes plagiarism; AI cannot be an author.
- **ICMJE (medical journal standards, broadly adopted):** AI cannot be an author.
- **Actual practice across 2023–2025:** I am not aware of a single Nature or Science paper that has credited an AI as above-the-fold author. Papers that used AI extensively (AlphaFold, protein design, AI-assisted discovery) consistently credit the human researchers, with AI use disclosed in methods sections.

For a YES resolution within the 70-day window remaining:
- Either the policy must have already quietly reversed AND a paper already published or in press with AI as author, OR
- A high-profile test-case paper is already in the pipeline

Neither of these is visible in the 2025–2026 discourse I have access to. Journal-policy reversal on author attribution is a major editorial decision that would generate substantial public debate; I would expect to know about it if it had happened.

Base-rate estimate: **~5%**, reflecting residual uncertainty about edge cases, special-issue papers, or policy announcements I might have missed.
