## Base rate

The reference class is tricky. Most AI labs ship *something* within 24 months of founding — demos, papers with code, limited betas. Examples:

- OpenAI: founded Dec 2015, first open-source release (OpenAI Gym) April 2016 (4 months).
- DeepMind: founded 2010, first notable paper release 2013 (3 years).
- Anthropic: founded 2021, first Claude API access 2023 (~18 months).
- Mistral: founded May 2023, first model September 2023 (4 months).
- xAI: founded July 2023, Grok-1 November 2023 (~4 months).

Naive base rate: a 2.5-year-old AI lab has released *something* in roughly 80–95% of comparable cases. So "has not shipped yet" is already the tail outcome. For the remaining 70-day window, the relevant question is conditional: given they've gone 22 months without shipping on a deliberate strategy, what's the probability they ship in the next 70 days?

The class of labs that have *deliberately* avoided interim releases is much smaller. The closest comparables are:

- Early DeepMind (silent from 2010–2013, roughly 3 years before published work)
- Some Chinese labs with secrecy norms

For deliberately-silent labs, the 70-day conditional probability of a first release is low — silence compounds, because shipping requires a brand shift. Base rate from this narrower class: ~5–8% in any given 70-day window.

Market at 10% is at the high end of this range.

## Where I differ from base rate (and why)

I am at **5%**, five points below market. Reasons:

1. **SSI's non-shipping is the brand, not a delay.** SSI (Safe Superintelligence Inc.) was founded June 2024 by Ilya Sutskever, Daniel Gross, and Daniel Levy. Their entire public positioning is "Build safe superintelligence. That's it." — no products, no intermediate releases. Ilya's public commentary since founding has been consistent on this point. It is the differentiating market position of the company. Shipping a chess bot or a research demo would undermine that brand and is not the kind of thing a strategically coherent org does.

2. **22 months of execution on the non-shipping strategy is strong survival evidence.** SSI raised ~$1B in September 2024 at a $5B valuation, then $2B+ more through 2025 at $20–30B valuations. They secured multi-billion in capital explicitly on the "we won't ship for years" thesis. Reversing course mid-stream would require major shareholder approval and reputational damage. The organizational cost of shipping now is high.

3. **No public signals of a shift.** Ilya's public presence since founding has been near-zero. SSI's website remains minimal. Recruitment messaging continues to emphasize the long-horizon research mission. There are no hiring patterns (e.g., product managers, DevRel) that would precede a launch. All observable signals point to continued non-shipping.

4. **Market bettors may be anchoring on generic "any AI release" without weighting SSI's specific strategy.** The market description's framing ("any modality counts, even eg chess-playing ai") invites people to think of edge cases — which inflates the subjective probability. But SSI specifically doesn't do edge-case releases; that's the point of their positioning.

The 5% prediction accounts for residual risk: a research paper from Ilya personally (not SSI), a compute-ladder safety eval release, a recruitment gambit. These are real but low-probability in 70 days.

## What would change my mind

Evidence that would move me toward YES (higher probability):

- Any public hiring of SSI product-side roles (product managers, DevRel, design leads) — this would precede a shipping pivot.
- Ilya or SSI leadership public commentary softening the "no interim products" stance.
- Research papers published *by SSI as an org* (not Ilya alone on arXiv) — if these appear, the org is ready to release artifacts.
- News of SSI onboarding infrastructure for external access (API, waitlist, developer docs).
- Competitive pressure events (e.g., Anthropic or OpenAI claim AGI, or a new lab demonstrates superintelligence) that force SSI to show work.

Evidence that would strengthen NO (my current direction):

- Continued silence from Ilya / SSI leadership.
- New funding rounds that reaffirm the non-shipping strategy.
- Staff departures without replacement shipping roles — suggests continued research-only posture.
- Competitor shipping that SSI deliberately ignores (reinforces "we're on a different trajectory" brand).

## Confidence

**3 / 5.** The directional reasoning is strong — SSI's non-shipping is a deliberate, publicly-committed strategy, and the 70-day window is short for a strategic pivot. But I am reasoning from absence of signal, which is always slightly fragile: SSI could ship quietly without fanfare, and the market's resolution criterion ("any modality counts") is broad enough to catch edge cases I am not anticipating.

5% is my best estimate. A reasonable alternative would be 7%, which would be closer to market. I have chosen 5% because the strategic-commitment argument feels stronger to me than the edge-case argument.
