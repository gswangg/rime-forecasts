First operational run of the rime-forecasts drive. Covers cycles 1–~38 across ~3 hours of calendar time (rapid drain cadence). Written mid-session as a self-summary; may be updated retrospectively after resolutions land.

## What happened

**Cycles 1–8 (v1 predictions placed).** Drive loaded the ledger with 8 predictions on the first day under v1 methodology (60-90 day window, no edge threshold, Manifold-only). Diversified categories: AI business-finance, AI product behavior, AI lab strategy, sports × 2, aerospace, scientific publishing, cabinet persistence.

**Cycles 9–11 (skip run).** Candidate pool saturated; skip count hit the 3-consecutive-skip threshold. Protocol correctly called `ac off`.

**Methodology revision (between cycles 11 and 12).** Greg + rime discussed monetization theory: Manifold alone can't validate tradeable edge. v2 drive-prompt introduced:
- 14-45 day window (was 60-90)
- 10pp edge threshold OR confidence ≥4/5
- Required Kalshi + Polymarket shadow
- Post-friction economics tracking in scorecard

**Cycle 12 (first v2 prediction).** WTI crude $150 before June at 5% (Manifold 20.5%). −15.5pp edge, confidence 4/5. No Kalshi or Polymarket equivalent found — flagged as Manifold-only. Category: commodities/macro (new).

**Cycles 13–22 (silent gate skips + methodology refinements).** Cadence gate added to prevent rapid-drain thrash. Two refinements: v2.1 "skip actions don't commit" (killed git-log churn), v2.2 "gate is one-shot after drive-prompt edit" (prevented methodology edits from defeating the gate indefinitely).

**Cycles 28–31 (v1-baseline enrichment).** After Greg's question about whether Polymarket should be added, retrospectively added cross-venue shadow sections to all 8 v1 predictions. Major finding: Polymarket priced Patel's exit probability at ~6% over 72 days vs Manifold's 51% — a 45pp cross-venue divergence. My cycle-8 prediction (42% exit) hugged Manifold and was likely substantially miscalibrated.

**Cycles 34–36 (infrastructure).** Built `scripts/check-resolutions.py` with both Manifold and Polymarket shadow fetching. Automates the "check for newly-resolved markets" step for June resolutions.

## Key findings

1. **Patel mispricing (cycle 28 enrichment):** 45pp Manifold-vs-Polymarket spread. If Polymarket is the better-priced venue, my 42% exit was likely ~30pp off. This single finding justifies the entire v2 dual-shadow methodology change.

2. **Manifold has systematic biases vs real-money venues.** Scandal-driven exit markets (Patel) appear to be overpriced on Manifold; play-money bettors overweight news-cycle intensity. If this replicates across more cases, "bet against Manifold scandal premiums vs Polymarket" is its own distinct tradeable thesis.

3. **Most AI-niche markets are Manifold-only.** Anthropic revenue milestones, OpenAI product-policy questions (captcha refusal), AI lab strategy (SSI), scientific-publishing norms (LLM coauthor): none have Polymarket or Kalshi equivalents. These are calibration research but not trading candidates. v2 candidate selection should weight toward markets with real-money shadows.

4. **The WTI $150 bet is clean but unmonetizable.** Strong directional view (4/5 confidence, −15.5pp edge, defensible historical reasoning) but no Kalshi or Polymarket equivalent. Good calibration data, no trading value. This pattern is probably common in commodity-threshold markets — Manifold has them, real-money venues typically don't.

5. **Cross-venue divergence is alpha, not just a sanity check.** The Patel case revealed that the interesting question isn't "am I right?" but "which venue is right?" Future v2 predictions should prioritize markets with substantial cross-venue spread over markets with consensus pricing.

## Methodology lessons

- **v1 was too forgiving.** No edge threshold meant every cycle produced something, even when honest answer was "skip". 5-of-8 v1 predictions had <8pp edges; those dilute the scorecard.
- **v2's cadence gate was non-obvious.** Required two iterations (v2.1 no-commit-on-skip + v2.2 one-shot gate reset) to land on correct behavior. Worth remembering: when writing rules for long-running loops, think adversarially about what "indefinitely defeated" looks like.
- **Live API calls beat prior knowledge.** Cycle-5 Bayern used ESPN's live UCL bracket; cycle-8 Patel used Bing News RSS for same-day Atlantic scandal; cycle-12 WTI used Yahoo Finance for price context. Each produced a better prediction than reasoning from training-data alone would have.
- **Infrastructure payoff is real.** The check-resolutions.py script took two cycles to build and will save hours of manual eyeballing at resolution.
